OMAR
Open Modular Access to Records
AI-connected VA Clinical Assistant.

What it is

  • Browser-based app with a clinician‑friendly workspace, based on CPRS.
  • AI‑powered assistant to help VA clinicians find and summarize chart information quickly.
  • AI‑powered note drafting with ambient transcription or dictation.

Who it’s for

  • Clinicians of all types that need quick patient context, deep chart exploration, and documentation drafting.
  • Designed to reduce cognitive load and documentation burden, allowing clinicians to focus on decision-making and patient care.
  • For those comfortable with CPRS. Intended to work alongside the EHR.

What it can do

  • CPRS sync: Syncs to CPRS even when on a remote desktop.
  • Question answering: Ask free‑text questions; answers show citations and date context.
  • Scribing support: Optional live transcription and draft note assistance.
  • Structured data: Dot‑phrase expander for quick labs/vitals/med lists or view in Snapshot tab.

How it works

  • Chunking: Section‑aware text processing; boilerplate detection and de‑duplication.
  • Retrieval: Keyword index + internal VA Azure OpenAI embeddings; hybrid ranking with diversity and mild recency weighting.
  • Note Drafting: VA Azure Speech-to-Text for transcription, coupled with default or custom prompts, and sent to internal VA Azure OpenAI model.

Data & interoperability

  • Works with VistA data. Outside records can be added manually for summarization.
  • Intended to be EHR-agnostic, eventually will be able to accept data in FHIR format from any source.

Privacy

  • Works only within the VA network.
  • All data remains within VA at all times, with AI calls using VA's internal Azure OpenAI model.
  • Embeddings and indices are kept in memory (LRU cache) only during a session.
  • Archived transcripts can be written to disk, but requires explicit configuration, and are deleted after set time.
  • Patient data is not used for model training or sent outside the clinician's environment.

What it doesn’t do (by default)

  • No background vector DB persistence out‑of‑the‑box (FAISS is in‑memory).
  • No automatic EHR write‑back—outputs are clinician‑facing summaries and drafts.

Quick start

  • Launch the app → select a patient.
  • Move tabs around per your preference
  • Open Snapshot for a fast overview (vitals, labs, meds, problems).
  • Ask a question in plain English → review answer with citations.
  • Press record to transcribe and to draft notes faster.
  • Explicit verbal consent from patients must be obtained and documented for any recording.

Safety & guardrails

  • Every answer cites the exact excerpts used.
  • Abnormal and critical lab values are visually distinguished.
  • Time‑sensitive statements include date context in prompts and outputs.
Warning: This product is in early development and has not been clinically validated. It cannot be relied upon for clinical care at this time. Restricted to test users only.
